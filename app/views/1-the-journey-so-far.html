How we've got to today

Shared Channels Experience was established in 2020 with a remit to truly understand DWP usersâ€™ needs and build solutions to meet them. Rather than focusing on individual benefits or policies, we wanted to concentrate on the overall experience DWP provides.

We know that 33% of our users claim more than one benefit from us. But we also know the services and platforms they use to access those services can look very different. This leads to uncertainty, confusion and the urge to pick up the phone to seek answers about a claim. To reassure those users and answer their questions before they call the department, we need more consistency across our services.

Among many initiatives, we looked at joining up how we send letters, emails and text messages to our users. We worked with the Government Digital Service to develop a better way for people to prove their identities when accessing government services. And we built up a detailed understanding of what user experiences could look like.

Lots of services, platforms and channels operate very well, but separately. Our job in Shared Channels Experience is to join them up and help our users give and receive information about their claims seamlessly.
